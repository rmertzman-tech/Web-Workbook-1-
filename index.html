Why "Ghost in the Machine" Doesn't Help

The traditional answer was to invoke something non-physical: "the soul," "consciousness," "free will," or what philosopher Gilbert Ryle critically called "the ghost in the machine."

But here's the problem: these labels don't actually explain anything‚Äîthey just name the mystery. Saying "free will did it" is like saying "magic did it." It doesn't tell us how the choosing happens, where it comes from, or how it's possible.

Even worse: These explanations stop us from investigating. If agency is some mystical, untouchable ghost, then there's nothing to study, nothing to understand. The inquiry ends before it begins.
